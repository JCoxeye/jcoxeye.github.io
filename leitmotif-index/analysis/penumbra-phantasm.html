JCoxeye's Leitmotif Index:
On Penumbra Phantasm

Preface

Penumbra Phantasm has quite the storied history - originally composed by Toby Fox for Homestuck, it went unused despite numerous renditions and iterations (and leaks therof).

Its motifs went on to be heard throughout Homestuck 's soundtrack, including (but not limited to) Savior of the Waking World and Umbral Ultimatum by Toby Fox, Renewed Return by Marcy Nabors, and Dogfight by various artists.
(Source: Penumbra Phantasm | HSMusic Wiki)

Now, with the release of Deltarune Chapters 1 and 2, some have noticed that the song's influence may not have ended with Homestuck - indeed, Penumbra Phantasm appears to have been referenced not only in Deltarune, but all the way back in Undertale, as well.

If you haven't heard it before (or haven't heard it in a while), I recommend listening to and getting acquainted with it:

Disclaimer:

Please do keep in mind three things:

  1. This index is incomplete, and is subject to change.
  2. Due to the nature of the topic at hand, whether or not any given melody is truly a quote of another is inherently subjective.

Use your own judgement when reading through, and especially if you plan to cite this index elsewhere!

Defining The Motifs

First, let's pick out the relevant portions of the song, and figure out what makes them identifiable.

The Opening Melody

This is the melody the song starts with, first in 3/4 time:

And then in 4/4 time:

Seeing how simple this motif is, to be confident in any quote, I'd need the exact intervals:
[-1 +3 -3 +0 +1, repeat]

The 4/4 variation's rhythm is notable too of course, with its noticeable syncopation, but in my opinion, Toby Fox doesn't tend to pay too much mind to rhythm when quoting leitmotifs, so I don't think it's necessary for prospective quotes to have the exact same rhythm.

The Main Melody

(The first part of it, at least)

Now this is a leitmotif!

It's based off of this motif:

[-1 +1 +2 -3 -4 -3]

It's repeated verbatim in the third measure, and in the second with a modification:

[-1 +1 +2 -3 -4 +12 -5 +2]

For this part of Penumbra Phantasm, I think the most relevant parts are...

  • The melody starts on an off-beat.
  • The [-1 +1] intervals of the opening few notes.
  • The [-3 -4 -3] intervals of the downwards motion.
  • The second phrase is modified, with a contour of [big leap -> normal descent -> small leap].

In any case, although the quote is perfect, the question remains: is it intentional?

In my opinion, yes.

These exact intervals with this exact rythm makes for too strong of a coincidence to ignore.

Additionally, consider that Toby Fox had been tweaking Penumbra Phantasm for years at this point, even going so far as to prank the Homestuck fanbase by dealing out fake 'leaks' of it.

Given this, it's difficult for me to argue that Toby composed this melody without noticing the striking resemblance.

The (first part of the) Main Melody as 'THE HOLY B'

Now, let's take a listen to the 'THE HOLY B' motif as heard in THE HOLY :

And here's the main motif of Penumbra Phantasm, for reference:

Let's revisit the four identifying features of Penumbra Phantasm 's main motif:

  1. The melody starts on an off-beat.
  2. The [-1 +1] intervals of the opening few notes.
  3. The [-3 -4 -3] intervals of the downwards motion.
  4. The second phrase is modified, with a contour of [big leap -> normal descent -> small leap].

First off, just like Penumbra Phantasm, 'THE HOLY B' starts on an off-beat.

Points 2 and 3 don't look so good at first glance, however:

(Penumbra Phantasm )

('THE HOLY B')

'THE HOLY B' is missing the [+1] and first [-3] intervals, in addition to a [+2] interval.

However, I think it's worth noting what happens when you remove the D, E, and C# notes from Penumbra Phantasm :

You get the same intervals as in 'THE HOLY B'.

Now of course, you can modify any motif to be any other motif, but I do think it's worth noting that 'THE HOLY B' may very well be a simplification of Penumbra Phantasm.

As for point 4, 'THE HOLY B' is indeed modified in its second phrase, in a way similar to Penumbra Phantasm:

(Penumbra Phantasm )

('THE HOLY B')

In fact, aside from intervals, the contour is identical, minus the extra F and E (which mind you, are repeats of the first two notes of the phrase).

Finally, it's worth noting that where Penumbra Phantasm has a third and fourth measure that further expands on the motif, 'THE HOLY B' does not have this.

Personally, I don't think it's too big of a deal, but it is worth mentioning.

Okay, so is this resemblance intentional?

Personally, I'm split:
There's more than enough similarities for this to be notable, but at the same time they're different enough for this to be doubtable.

As unfortunate as it is for me to say this, I think we'll just have to wait for Chapters 3 and 4 to see where the 'THE HOLY B' motif ends up next.

After all, THE HOLY is the only definitive quote we have of it; it's possible future renditions will resemble Penumbra Phantasm more closely (or not).
