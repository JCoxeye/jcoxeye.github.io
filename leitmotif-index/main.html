Preface

Toby Fox's Undertale uni(multi?)verse uses leitmotifs A leitmotif is a repeated musical expression (melody, instrument, rhythm, etc.) found throughout a particular work. to establish themes and connections, and to enhance the plot and themes of the story.

This index aims to catalogue said usage of leitmotifs to the best of my ability. In doing so, I hope to create a comprehensive resource for anyone to reference in regards to theorycrafting and other discussion.

Of course, I am far from the first to attempt this, and I would like to give thanks to those who I can base and double-check my work off of:

MediaMotifs,

and all of the contributors to the Undertale and Deltarune Wikias.

I'd also like to personally thank the Andrew Cunningham Discord Server for helping to make this index a reality.

In particular, I thank Awesome Possum and Trondor for their participation in leitmotivic discussion and analysis.

Disclaimer:

Please do keep in mind three things:

  1. This index is incomplete, and is subject to change.
  2. Due to the nature of the topic at hand, whether or not any given melody is truly a quote of another is inherently subjective.
  3. Most importantly, I have no formal education in music theory, so don't take my partially-educated ramblings as gospel!

Use your own judgement when reading through, and especially if you plan to cite this index elsewhere!

(Also, feel free to contact me via discord or e-mail to discuss/correct any disagreements/inaccuracies/typos you find)

Info

Throughout this index, in order to ease confusion when talking about songs versus motifs, song names are italicized, whereas the names of leitmotifs are put in single quotes, like so:

The 'Ruins' leitmotif first makes its appearance in the song Ruins.
'Ruins' later appears in Waterfall alongside 'Another Medium'.

The index itself is split into various articles:

  • Leitmotif articles, which cover specific leitmotifs
  • Analysis articles, which cover specific points of analysis

Right now, this index is in its early stages, and therefore only contains rough overviews of most leitmotifs in the Undertale and Deltarune soundtracks, with little analysis.
