JCoxeye's Leitmotif Index:
'Undyne'

Summary


('Undyne', as heard in Spear of Justice)

Undyne's theme.
It's heard in most every song relating to her, and as such is often used alongside 'Ruins'.

In Dating Start! and Undyne, Undyne also has a different, more 'spooky' melody:


('Undyne (Tense)' overtop of 'Undyne', as heard in Undyne)

Appearances

Dating Tense!

As opposed to the other dating themes, 'Snowy' and 'Hopes and Dreams' are absent from Dating Tense!, instead replace by 'Undyne':

'Undyne (Tense)': 0:08-0:19
'Undyne': 0:17-0:25

Undyne

'Undyne': 0:00-0:37

'Undyne (Tense)': 0:12-0:25

In Undyne, the motif has a consistent, almost militaristic rhythm, as opposed to the more bombastic rhythm as heard in Spear of Justice.
Dating Start! and Run! are evidently based off of Undyne, as they too have this consistent rhythm.

Run!

'Undyne': 0:00-0:15

Listening to Run!, your first thought may be that it does not have a consistent rhythm.

However, upon closer inspection, the lopsided feel of the song is primarily delivered via a bizarre syncopation to 4/4 time, as opposed to a nonstandard rhythm.

Three measures of 'Undyne' in 3/4 time are crammed into two measures of 4/4 time, prematurely starting the next phrase.

For a more in-depth analysis of this syncopation (and more analysis on the motif in general), I highly recommend Alex Yard's video on the subject.

NGAHHH!!

'Undyne': 0:00-0:34

In NGAHHH!! and Spear of Justice, the motif is given a more bombastic rhythm, of course reflecting Undyne's true personality.
Its last measure is also changed to an extension of the previous measure, fleshing out the melody.

(See also: 'Ruins')

Spear of Justice

'Undyne': 0:00-0:29

As the song transitions into the 'Ruins' motif, the ending of the melody introduced in NGAHHH!! is extended:

'Undyne': 0:30-0:36

undyne battle (unused)
