JCoxeye's Leitmotif Index:
'Once Upon a Time'

Summary


(The three parts of 'Once Upon a Time', as heard in Once Upon a Time)

'Once Upon a Time' acts as Undertale's main theme, appearing in nearly twenty songs, in addition to a few in Deltarune.

It first appears in (of course) Once Upon a Time, and is later featured in numerous songs, including the True Pacifist route's final boss themes, Hopes and Dreams and SAVE the World.

Appearances

Once Upon a Time

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:00-0:28

Once 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2' kicks in, so too does a simplified version of 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3':

'Once Upon a Time, Parts 2 and 3': 0:29-0:58

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 0:58-1:27

Start Menu

'Once Upon a Time, part 1': 0:00-0:19

As the game progresses and more monsters are befriended, the menu is filled up with characters, and Start Menu is fleshed out with more instruments.
Aside from Menu (Full), which is covered below, they're mostly identical in terms of leitmotifs, and will not be covered here.

Undertale

Overtop of 'Memory', Undertale primarily features 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:37-2:49, 3:26-4:42

(See also: 'Home')

At 2:49, the song temporarily drops the 'Memory' motif, and the melody changes to 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 2': 2:49-3:26

From there, 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1' resumes until 4:42, at which point the melody progresses to different version of the motif, reminiscent of Asgore's theme, 'Bergentr√ºckung':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 4:42-5:20

Finally, 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1' returns in its standard form, transposed up a semitone from C Minor into E Major, and with a few small modifications:

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 5:20-5:57

undertale (unused)

Overtop a version of 'Home', undertale (unused) features 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1', just like the final version with 'Memory':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:32-2:40

Just like the final, undertale (unused) also drops 'Home' upon progressing to 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 2': 2:40-3:12

Finally, unlike the final version of the song, 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2' stays put, while 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3' backs it up, like in Once Upon a Time's middle section:

'Once Upon a Time, Parts 2 and 3': 3:12-4:00

The Choice

The Choice is timestamps 2:30-2:47 of Undertale slowed to 15% speed (with some effects added):

Speeding it back up reveals 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1' and 'Memory':

Fallen Down (Reprise)

Following a reprise of Toriel's Theme, 'Fallen Down', 'Once Upon a Time' is syncopated into a 3/4 time signature to fit in with the rest of the song:

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 1:18-1:44

'Once Upon a Time, Part 2': 1:44-2:10

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 2:10-2:30

Hopes and Dreams

First, Hopes and Dreams starts out with a violin rendition of 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:00-0:22

Before leading into an electric guitar-backed chiptune rendition:

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:22-0:44

From there, the song does roughly the same with 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 0:44-1:07

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 1:07-1:29

After a quote of Flowey's theme, 'Your Best Friend', 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3' is transposed up a semitone from C Minor into E Major, like 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1' in Undertale:

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 1:52-2:14

Finally, the song closes on a quote of 'Hopes and Dreams'.

SAVE the World

In SAVE the World, 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1' similarly plays in the opening of the song, interspliced with 'Your Best Friend':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:00-0:05, 0:10-0:15

After doing the same with 'Home', the song then quotes 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3', in a manner similar to Hopes and Dreams:

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 0:42-1:46

Finally, the song ends on a quote of 'Your Best Friend'.

Similar to The Choice, Final Power is a portion of Hopes and Dreams, reversed, slowed, and with added echo:

Reunited

Reunited first quotes 'Once Upon a Time, Part 1' over a backing reminiscent of 'Another Medium':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:44-1:43

Before quoting 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 2': 1:43-2:42

And then 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 2:57-3:26

Before remixing it and upping the intensity of the instrumentation, like in Hopes and Dreams and SAVE the World:

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 3:26-3:55

Finally, like Hopes and Dreams, the song quotes the 'Hopes and Dreams' motif leading into its coda.

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 1:29-1:34

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 1:53-2:15

You Can Always Come Home

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:19-0:56

Despite being a reprise of Home, You Can Always Come Home doesn't contain 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2', as it's replaced by 'Don't Forget B', backed up by 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3':

'Once Upon a Time, Part 3': 1:19-1:33

(In the bells(?) in the back, panned right)

This quote of 'Once Upon a Time, Part 3' and 'Don't Forget B' are both cut off by a quote of 'Hometown (Extra)'.

Before the Story

'Once Upon a Time, Part 1': 0:29-1:28

Note that despite its use in Deltarune (and the apparent presence of the 'THE HOLY' leitmotif), this song actually originates from the Undertale PS4 theme.

Dog Check

Since 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2' isn't present in You Can Always Come Home, its only appearance in Deltarune's soundtrack thus far is in this six second portion of the error handler screen music.

As a 'Once Upon a Time, Part 2' enjoyer, I'm outraged.

'Once Upon a Time, Part 2': 0:27-0:33
