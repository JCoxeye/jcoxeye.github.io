JCoxeye's Leitmotif Index:
'Your Best Friend'

Summary


('Your Best Friend', as heard in Your Best Friend)

'Your Best Friend' is the leitmotif of Flowey.

Notably, it appears in Deltarune exactly once: in the honks heard during the traffic jam at the beginning of Chapter 2.
This is distinctly opposed to 'Your Best Nightmare', which functions as Flowey's secondary motif in Undertale, but is given new life in multiple Deltarune tracks.

Finally, it's worth mentioning that 'Your Best Friend' is likely inspired by the F.U.N. Song from Spongebob Squarepants, as discussed on the page 'On the Origins of Undertale and Deltarune's Leitmotifs'.

Appearances

Your Best Friend

'Your Best Friend': 0:07-0:22

mus_toomuch.ogg

mus_toomuch.ogg is the music that plays upon encountering the 'But nobody came.' message after exhausting an area's kill count (most prominently seen during the Genocide/No Mercy route).

Speeding it up to 13.333x speed reveals it's the first quarter of the 'Your Best Friend' motif.

(Be warned, it's a little tough on the ears)

Your Best Nightmare

In the files, Your Best Nightmare is split into numerous different sound files, which in the soundtrack manifests as various distinct 'sections' of the song, namely six SOUL Phases, alongside some transitory phases.

First, the six SOUL Phases:

'Your Best Friend': 0:59-1:09

'Your Best Friend': 1:15-1:25

'Your Best Friend': 2:01-2:11

'Your Best Friend': 2:17-2:27

'Your Best Friend': 3:00-3:10

'Your Best Friend': 3:16-3:26

And finally, the transitory phase between the Orange SOUL Phase and Blue SOUL Phase, which would normally contain 'Your Best Nightmare' as indicated by the structure of the song, instead appears to contain a heavily distorted version of the 'Your Best Friend' motif:

'Your Best Friend': 1:26-1:47

(Above: 'Your Best Friend'; below: Your Best Nightmare, 1:26-1:47)

As you can see, overall, they really look nothing alike.

Looking into the specifics however, there are some similarities.

Namely, the descending sequence at the end of the first half of the Your Best Nightmare quote [C6 -> G5 -> D5] is very similar to the descending sequence in the first half of 'Your Best Friend' [B5 -> F#5 -> B4].

The descent isn't quite identical (it's only a 11 semitone descent instead of a full octave), but it's very similar nonetheless.

Further, the slow build up at the very end of the Your Best Nightmare quote [B5 -> C6 -> C#6] is somewhat similar to the foundation of the 'Your Best Friend' motif, the [G#5 -> G#5 -> A5 -> B5].

Notably though, 'Your Best Friend's [G# -> A5 -> B5] intervals are intervals of [+1 +2] as opposed to [+1 +1].

These two factors combine to make something that vaguely sounds like 'Your Best Friend', whilst not really being the same.

Ultimately, I conclude that this is an intentional allusion to the motif, but perhaps not a true quote thereof.

Finale

'Your Best Friend': 0:00-0:40, 0:57-1:52

mus_f_finale_1 is an unused, shorter version of mus_f_finale_1_l, the first sound file that makes up Finale in-game (and the one shown above).
It's not really any different, and so it won't be covered here.
