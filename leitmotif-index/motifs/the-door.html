JCoxeye's Leitmotif Index:
'The Door'

Summary


('The Door', as heard in The Chase)

'The Door' is a seldom-used motif that appears to have been lifted from an earlier Toby Fox song, Nightmare Knight (no relation).
(probably)

In any case, despite being used in so few songs, the motif is somewhat commonly brought up in lore discussion, primarily because of Nightmare Knight, which has led some to believe that the motif is related to the Knight them(it?)self.

Appearances

The Door

First, the song starts out with a simplified version of the motif:

'The Door': 0:00-0:13

Before progressing to the complete melody:

'The Door': 0:13-0:41

The Chase

'The Door': 0:11-0:33

THE WORLD REVOLVING

'The Door': 0:02-0:10, 0:12-0:20

(See also: 'THE WORLD REVOLVING' and 'Don't Forget')

Checkerboard

Checkerboard (an unused song) doesn't contain the full 'The Door' leitmotif, but it does contain what appears to be the simplied version of the motif as heard in The Door :

'The Door': 0:11-0:35, 0:47-1:35

('The Door', as heard in The Door, for reference):

Nightmare Knight

No, Deltarune Chapter 7 did not secretly leak.

In fact, although it was composed by Toby Fox, this song actually has next to nothing to do with Deltarune - it was a fan song composed for Cucumber Quest, which is a web comic written by Gigi D.G., who is also credited for various pieces of concept art for Deltarune.

Nightmare Knight was intended to be a theme for the comic's main antagonist, the Nightmare Knight (who may or not have inspired the 'Knight Knight' enemy from Undertale).

In any case, Nightmare Knight appears to be the origin of the 'The Door' motif; take a listen:

'The Door': 0:48-1:11

Later on in the song, the melody is played in double-time, and slightly remixed:

'The Door': 1:30-1:53

As an aside, if this all sounds familiar, it's because Toby Fox has done this before:

  • 'Metal Crusher' originates from Noisemaster
  • 'THE HOLY' originates from Penumbra Phantasm
  • 'Another Medium' originates from Patient
