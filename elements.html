To the average player of Deltarune, combat is simple.

Hit the enemy, their HP decreases.

Get hit by the enemy, your HP decreases.

But, on closer examination, something interesting comes to light:

Deltarune has an Element System.

Indeed, if you were to wear the Frayed Bowtie, Mannequin, or Dealmaker, you'd find yourself taking less damage against Tasque, Tasque Manager, and Spamton NEO.

Similarly, the recruits page informs you of the Elements of each recruited enemy.

This seems like something we can barely begin to speculate on, but we can shed more light on it by looking through the code for what exactly is going on (see below!).

What We Know So Far

First, let's list all of the Elements found on the recruits page:

  • Jewel (Rudinn and Rudinn Ranger)
  • Heart (Hathy and Head Hathy)
  • Order (Ponmen, Ambyu-Lance, and Tasque Manager)
  • Rabbit (Rabbick)
  • Dust (Rabbick)
  • Fight (Bloxer and Werewerewire)
  • Mouse (Jigsawry, Maus, and Mauswheel
  • Puzzle (Jigsawry)
  • Blade (Rudinn Ranger)
  • Ice (Head Hathy)
  • Elec (Ambyu-Lance, Tasque, Werewire, Maus, and Werewerewire
  • Virus (Poppup and Virovirokun)
  • Cat (Tasque and Tasque Manager)
  • Color (Swatchlings)
  • Chaos (Jevil's unused recruits entry)

Additionally, Rude and Fire can be assumed to exist through Susie's Rude Buster spell (described as dealing 'moderate Rude-elemental damage') and that Toriel is near-guaranteed to use Fire magic.

Next, let's take a look at gml_GlobalScript_scr_armorinfo, the code that determines the effects of armor items:

This script defines the properties of every armor item in the game, including unused armors.

I'll spare you the pain of a gigantic image of it all, and simply summarize it's most notable features for our purposes:

  • 'MouseToken':
    "A golden coin with a once-powerful mousewizard engraved on it."
    'armorelementtemp' = 7
    'armorelementamounttemp' = 0.5

  • 'Mannequin':
    "It's a mannequin with the clothes permanently attached. Useless"
    'armorelementtemp' = 6
    'armorelementamounttemp' = 0.35

  • 'SkyMantle':
    "A cape that shimmers fluorescently. Protects against Elec and Holy attacks."
    'armorelementtemp' = 1
    'armorelementamounttemp' = 0.5

  • 'FrayedBowtie':
    "An old bowtie. It seems to have lost much of its defensive value."
    'armorelementtemp' = 6
    'armorelementamounttemp' = 0.15

  • 'Dealmaker':
    "Fashionable pink and yellow glasses. Greatly increase $ gained, and...?"
    'armorelementtemp' = 6
    'armorelementamounttemp' = 0.4

As you can see, the Frayed Bowtie, Mannequin, and Dealmaker all have Element 6, the unused Mouse Token has Element 7, and the unused Sky Mantle has Element 1.

Additionally, the Sky Mantle's description says it defends against Elec and Holy attacks, despite only seeming to defend against Element 1.

From this, we can conclude that Elements are listed in an internal numerical index:

  1. Mouse is defined as Element 7
  2. Elec and Holy are defined as Element 1, as part of some sort of Elemental Pair
  3. Tasque, Tasque Manager, and Spamton NEO, as well as the Frayed Bowtie, Mannequin, and Dealmaker, are defined as Element 6
  4. As the only known Element that any of these have in common is Cat, we can conclude that Cat is defined as Element 6

Where in the code is this supposed 'Elemental Index', anyway?

To answer those questions, let's take a look at gml_GlobalScript_scr_element_damage_reduction:

This script is called in the general damage calculation script, and is the closest we have to an Elemental Index.

When called, this script gives returns a damage multiplier value ('___reduction').

All of the damage you take is multiplied by this number, which is 1 by default.

If you have an item that has an Elemental Resistance, the game will reduce the oncoming damage according to the formula seen above.

It's kinda hard to tell what's going on there, so I'll summarize:

  • The amount an equipped armor item with Elemental Resistance reduces an attack's damage by is equal to ('armorelementamounttemp' × 100)%

  • Elemental Resistances stack additively, with a maximum damage reduction of 75%

  • Attacks of Elements 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7 will have their damage reduced if you're wearing an item of that same Element, with no special conditions

  • When you're damaged by an Element 0 attack, the Elemental Resistance equation is skipped entirely, meaning it can't be resisted no matter what armor you have
    Like Element 6, this Element is used in-game, as all overworld bullets are set to use Element 0.

  • Element 2 and 8 attacks are resisted by not only their own Elements, but also Element 9
    (to be clear, if you wear an item of Element 2, you'll resist Element 2; if you you wear an item of Element 8, you'll resist Element 8; if you wear an item of Element 9, you'll resist Elements 2, 8, and 9)

  • Finally, armor that resists against Element 10 will also defend against attacks of any Element, except for Element 0

Now we can get to the Deltarune 6th Anniversary Spamton Sweepstakes lore.

Near the end of the sweepstakes, 'Spamton' gave answers to select questions forwarded to the Fangamer Twitter account, and gave numerous insights into himself and related lore, including Deltarune's Element System:

From this, we gained three pieces of crucial information:

  1. The Dark, Star, Death, Scythe, and Puppet Elements were confirmed to exist

These Elements correspond to an internal index of 11 Elements:

  • Elements 3, 4, and 5, which have no known special properties

Death/Scythe is a known pair that Spamton finds especially dangerous.

Fire and Ice

Fire and Ice are extremely archetypal Elements, often considered the opposites of each other. In JRPGs such as Final Fantasy, Chrono Trigger, and even Pokemon, Fire, Ice, and Electric are considered to be a trio.

Of course, in Deltarune, Elec is already paired with Holy, and so Fire and Ice are more likely to be paired or otherwise related to each other.

Fire and Ice often represent opposing ideas: Fire is chaotic, destructive, loud, and energetic, while Ice is structured, solid, contemplative, and lethargic; provided they aren't paired with each other, Fire and Ice are likely to be paired with another couple of unpaired Elements that are similarly opposed or related, such as:

  • Chaos and Order
  • Fight and Puzzle
  • Heart and Jewel
  • Rude and Blade
    • (Chaos, Fight, Heart, and Rude are passionate and loud Elements, similar to Fire; Order, Puzzle, Jewel, and Blade are structured, orderly, and contemplative Elements, similar to Ice)

Elec

Elec is yet another achetypal Element, usually grouped with Fire and Ice in a trio. It's already paired with Holy, but perhaps we can still find some useful information by observing its relation to the Holy Element.

Its pairing with Holy seems rather arbitrary, but going back to JRPG archetypes, lightning and thunder magic is often associated with the sky and the heavens, particularly in Chrono Trigger (a major inspiration for Deltarune), where lightning and heavenly magic were combined into 'Light' ('天', roughly 'Heaven' or 'Sky') magic.

Even in more western cultures, thunder and lightning are commonly associated with godly power, such as Thor or Zues, who are the gods of thunder in their respective religions. Thor in particular is a hallowing god, whereas Zues is also the god of the sky.

...Okay, perhaps that wasn't the most important thought exercise in the world, but at least you may have learned something new!

Holy

Straying into less common Elements, we have Holy, which presumeably pertains to the sky and other heavenly concepts.

The concept of holiness is a prevalent theme in Deltarune, with King describing the Knight as the 'Holy Knight, who pulls the fountains from the Earth.'. In fact, the entire Dark World is often decribed as being Holy, from the fountains' song THE HOLY, to the 'ANGEL'S HEAVEN' that Ralsei mentions.

With that, Holy definitely seems to be a counterpart to Dark, especially given that Chrono Trigger's combination of lightning and heavenly magic is translated as 'Light' magic.

Given that both of these Elements already have pairs, perhaps these pairs will be related to each other somehow? The unused Sky Mantle item protects against Holy and Elec attacks, so perhaps the Shadow Mantle Seam mentions can be upgraded to or from it, and protects against Dark and Star attacks.

Dark

Speaking of, Dark is an Element we know to exist via Spamton's QnA answer on Elements.

It likely refers to, well, darkness.

Unanimously, in all cultures, the Dark is commonly understood to be dangerous and undesirable, or at the very least inconvenient.

Considering this alongside Deltarune's fixation on the concept of darkness, Dark seems like it may be a more powerful Element, almost certainly wielded by the Knight and the main bosses of each chapter.

Dark is likely antithetical or otherwise related to Holy as discussed previously, and is confirmed to be paired with the Star Element.

It also seems like it would fit well alongside Death, Chaos, or even Puppet (which would reflect the lack of freedom experienced by dark worlds and their occupants), but as Dark is already paired with Star, these correlations are likely little more than footnotes, especially given that the aforementioned connection with Holy is far more likely to be expanded upon.

Star

Star is one of the more confounding Elements:

It's seemingly unrelated to its partner Dark, it's far removed from what one would normally classify as an 'Element', and it's hard to imagine how such an Element would actually appear or be used in-game, barring truly apocolyptic circumstances.

All that can really be said about it just based off of its name is that it's likely a more powerful Element, which lines up with its partner Dark.

Its relation to Dark may be related to the fact that Stars are the universe's only major light sources; they produce light and are surrounded by darkness.

In Undertale however, Stars are of particular importance.
A room near the Snowdin end of Waterfall has a plaque on the wall reading 'WISHING ROOM'.
The echo flowers in the room relay:

"* A long time ago, monsters would whisper their wishes to the stars in the sky."
"* If you hoped with all your heart, your wish would come true."
"* Now, all we have are these sparkling stones on the ceiling..."

"* Thousands of people wishing together can't be wrong!"
"* The king will prove that."

Reinforcing the themes prevalent in Waterfall, Stars represent Hope to the monsters of the underground. They represent a future in which they can live on the surface in peace.

Now, consider this.

If we think of Star as representing Hope, then what does Dark represent?

Dreams.

From Queen's dialogue to the dialogue of the Rudinn in Castle Town, Deltarune repeatedly compares the Dark World to Dreams.

Indeed, I firmly believe that Dark/Star is analagous to Undertale's themes of Hopes and Dreams.
Of course, it doesn't take a genius to figure out that I also think that the Dark/Star pair belongs to Element 10, but we'll discuss that later.

Death/Scythe

Death and Scythe are an Elemental Pair introduced by the Deltarune 6th Anniversary Spamton Sweepstakes.

I honestly don't have much to say regarding either of these on their own, as they seem like a reasonable pair, with little connection to any other Elements.

One thing to mention is that Scythe is seemingly meant to be Jevil's Element, despite the fact that Jevil's Elements are supposedly Chaos and, um, Chaos.

We can assume this via the fact that:

  1. Jevil is the only one shown to wield a Scythe thus far
  2. In mentioning the pair, Spamton seems especially wary of the pair, which may tie in to his dislike of Jevil

Notably, Spamton's warning may hint at a special property of the pair:

"WITH AN [Valuable item], YOU CAN DEFEND AGAINST [Puppet/Cat] OR [Thunder/Light], [Dark/Star]... JUST WATCH OUT FOR THE [$#&*] THAT USE [Death/Scythe]!!! NEVER AGAIN!!!!"

Death/Scythe seems to be juxtaposed with the concept of Elemental Resistance, which may imply that Death/Scythe is the identity of Element 0 (which cannot be resisted against, if you recall).

Thematically, this makes sense, as Element 0 would have to be a relatively dangerous Element to bypass Elemental Resistance like that.

Jewel

Jewel, the signature Element of Rudinn and Rudinn Ranger, seems to be a counterpart to Heart (the signature Element of Hathy and Head Hathy).

Aside from fitting in with Heart via a card motif, Jewel may also be related to Ice (a crystalline structure similar to Jewels) or Order (as Jewels and crystals are defined by their ordered molecular structure).

Color (an Element I find difficult to pair with any other Elements) may also be related Jewel, as Jewels are often prized specifically for their resplendance and Color.

With all of that said, I still find it likely that:

  1. Heart and Jewel are paired together...
  2. ...in the event they're even present in the actual Element System at all

Indeed, there are quite a few elements (particularly of the following few) that are of questionable relevance.

Star may be all but confirmed to appear as an actual Element because of Spamton's specific mention of Resistance against it, but in what situation would 'Rabbit' or 'Dust' or 'Jewel' or 'Heart' ever be relevant?

After all, given Toby Fox's track record regarding in-game statistics (see: Glyde's statistics being 'ATK: HIGH DEF: HIGH'), it's entirely possible these 'Elements', though hypothetically existant in-universe, are irrelevant to actual gameplay.

Jewel, the others I mentioned, and even Elements like Fight and Puzzle may be simple flavor text, and nothing more.

Jewel may be related to Heart, Ice, or Order.

Heart

Of the two card-originated Elements, Heart seems to be a little more relevant.

After all, SOULs are represented by Hearts, and Hearts are often associated with things like LOVE and the like.

With that said, it is important to note that, like Jewel, it's entirely possible that Heart has no actual relevance aside from flavor text.

As mentioned earlier, Heart may be connected to the Fire Element via the concept of 'burning passion'

Alternatively, Heart may be associated with Ice via the idea of being 'cold' to others (as seen by the behaviors of Head Hathy or Noelle during the Snowgrave route).

Puppet could also be related to Heart, given that the connection between Kris and The Player is via Kris's Heart-shaped SOUL; Spamton even mentions Kris being a 'HEART ON A CHAIN'.

If we want to go super hard on the SOUL theming, we could even relate it to Color, given how the Color of SOULs is pretty important to Undertale, and likely to Deltarune as well.

Heart may be related to Jewel, Fire, Ice, Puppet, or Color.

Order and Chaos

Order and Chaos are obviously related; given certain in-game interactions (namely Tasque Manager's reaction to Jevil's items) as well as simple cultural context, they serve as opposites and/or counterparts to eachother.

The divide of Order/Chaos is often associated with the divides of calmness/energy, strictness/laissez faire attitudes, organization/disorganization, predictability/unpredictability, and stability/destruction.

With that, Chaos may be related to Fire or Fight, whereas Order may be related to Ice, Puzzle, or Jewel.

Of course, Jewel is the outlier here; Jewel's assumed counterpart, Heart, doesn't seem to have much to do with Chaos at first glance, although one could argue something regarding the unpredictability of human emotions.

Of course, Order and Chaos may be paired with any pair of the Elements above, with eachother, or with entirely unrelated Elements.
In my opinion though, Fire/Chaos and Ice/Order seem the most likely, especially considering the ideas floated by the characters of Susie and Ralsei.

Speaking of, Susie and Ralsei feel exceptionally relevant to the Elements System, given the variety of characteristics attributed to them:

Susie is loud, disorderly, and chaotic, can use the Fire-adjacent Half-warm ACT against Swatchlings, and is referred to as a 'Dark Knight' who attacks using 'Dark Energy'.

Ralsei is calm, orderly, and polite, can use the Ice-adjacent Half-cold ACT against Swatchlings, and is referred to as the 'Prince of Darkness'.

Susie represents the 'dark' half of the tao: Chaos, Fire, and Fight suit her well.

Ralsei represents the 'light' half of the tao: Order, ice, and Puzzle fit him quite nicely.

Rude seems to fit in very well with Chaos. In fact, Jevil has closer connections to Susie than the other party members, as both are hyped for the battle, ready to go 'SHARK TO SHARK!'.

Like Jewel from earlier however, Rude seems an odd choice:
Its closest comparison, Blade (presumably the Element of Kris and their attacks), doesn't seem to have much relation to Order.

Hypothetically, Blade can be stretched to fit the description of Order, as it evokes images of smooth, well-defined Blades used by orderly Knights and the like.

Still, it's a vague connection, and arguably pokes a bit of a hole in this relation.

To Recap:

Chaos relates to Fire, Fight, Rude, and arguably Heart.

Order relates to Ice, Puzzle, Jewel, and arguably Blade.

Fight and Puzzle

Like Order and Chaos, Fight and Puzzle seem highly related to each other.

In Undertale, Fighting and Puzzling were opposed to each other via the dichotemy of murder and mercy, and Susie and Ralsei seem to uphold this dichotemy in Deltarune.

Further, Fight and Puzzle tie in to the dichotemy of Order and Chaos present in Deltarune's Element System, as discussed in the sub-section on that pair.

Regardless of this dichotemy, Fight may relate to energetic, active, and offensive Elements such as Fire, Rude, Blade, Scythe, and of course Chaos.

Opposingly, Puzzle may relate to contemplative or otherwise calmer Elements such as Order or Ice (a connection notable given Noelle's habit of freezing puzzles to skip them).

Breaking further away from the dichotemy of Order and Chaos, Puzzle may relate to Jewel, Heart, and/or Color, relating more to literal physical Puzzles as opposed to vague ideas.
Of course, in Deltarune's universe, such puzzle would also inclued Mice

To Recap:

Fight may relate to Fire, Chaos, Rude, Blade, or Scythe.

Puzzle may relate to Ice, Order, Jewel, Heart, Color, or Mouse.

Cat and Puppet

These two are the Elements we know the most about, and it's by far.

We know their pairing, their ID, and the lack of special properties the Elements System gives them.

Really, all that's left is figure out why.

Why exactly are Cat and Puppet a pair?

Well, given the obvious implication behind a Puppet Element, one can assume it has something to do with the concept of freedom.

Thinking about this, one notes that Cats are often free to do as they please, whereas Mice (which we already know to be separate from this pair) are subject to the Cats' whims:
Mice are the Puppets, and Cats are the Puppet masters.

This seems to contradict the basis of such a pairing, but now look towards how Cats are presented in Deltarune, specifically in the Cyber World.

Tasques are managed by Tasque Manager; they are subject to her training and Orders, whereas the Maice run wild in the alleyways and in Queen's mansion, effectively free from anyone's control.

Now this is a bit more sensible, isn't it?

Considering this, what else may we glean from connections such as this?

One would expect a future Element to be introduced to mirror Puppet, and for it to be paired with Cat.

I, uh, don't quite know what such an Element would be; my only guess would be 'Strings' or the like.

Mouse tails kinda look like string, and it would work if we take 'Strings' as representing the Puppet master pulling them.

Similar to Death/Scythe, there's not much more to say, as there aren't too many free Elements that could relate to either Cat nor Puppet, aside from Mouse and a hypothetical opposite of Puppet.

Mouse

Mouse, belonging to the Element 7 slot, is pretty clearly the opposite of Cat in some way, given the trope of Cats chasing Mice, as well as opposing the ideas presented by Puppet, as discussed on the section on that pair.

With that said, we can get a bit of an idea of what exactly the Mouse Element is about by comparing and contrasting it with Cat and Puppet.

Mouse may relate to ideas of freedom or the like, in addition to being a small (and often dirty) prey animal often used in Puzzles.

With that, Mouse may relate to Rabbit, Virus or Dust, or a hypothetical Element opposite to Puppet.

Rabbit

In many cultures, Rabbits relate to ideas of purity, serenity, nature, or the moon.

These ideas may suggest-okay whatever let me get to the point.

I don't think Rabbit is of any real significance.

It might be paired with another small animal such as Mouse or hypothetical Elements such as 'Frog' or 'Dog' or whatnot.

Aside from that, I find it difficult to really relate Rabbit to anything aside from Dust, which is likely only because of Rabbick.

Even then, Dust is more likely to be paired with Virus.
